## 1. Executive Summary

The contemporary landscape of cyberinfrastructure is undergoing a paradigm shift from static, hardware-defined architectures to dynamic, software-defined, and increasingly autonomous environments. The NSF-funded FABRIC testbed—of which the University of Kentucky is a founding partner—represents the vanguard of this transition, providing an "everywhere-programmable" research infrastructure where the network is treated as part of the computing continuum rather than a passive conduit for data transfer [Baldin et al., 2020].

However, the management of transcontinental FABRIC "slices" and the enforcement of network security policies across international boundaries remain manually intensive processes. Dr. Zongming Fei's recent work on POLANCO and the Network Policy Conversation Engine (NPCE) has identified this challenge precisely: the translation of high-level institutional policies into low-level SDN states is "tedious, manual, and error-prone" [Fei et al., 2021; Shi & Fei, 2022].

This proposal presents **OSIRIS** (Orchestrated Sovereign Intelligence for Research Infrastructure Systems)—a biological programming middleware layer that transforms FABRIC experiments into self-healing, self-monitoring **Living Slices**. By modeling network nodes as "organs" of a single distributed organism, OSIRIS achieves:

1. **Autonomous slice provisioning** via Non-Causal Intent Extraction, eliminating manual configuration
2. **Real-time entropy suppression** using phase-conjugate healing derived from the Cognitively Resonant Spacetime Manifold (CRSM) framework
3. **POLANCO policy upcycling** — transforming static firewall rules into Living Security Organisms that autonomously detect, heal, and adapt to adversarial conditions
4. **Immutable experiment provenance** via automated Zenodo archival with DOI minting

Preliminary quantum circuit benchmarks on IBM Brisbane and Torino backends demonstrate sustained cross-entropy benchmarking (XEB) fidelity of 0.867 ± 0.012 at 8 qubits scaling to 0.834 ± 0.014 at 16 qubits across 30 validated jobs, establishing the computational substrate for network-resident quantum optimization.

The proposed partnership leverages Dr. Fei's leadership in FABRIC and FABRIC Across Borders (FAB), his expertise in SDN and multicast delivery, and the University of Kentucky's ecosystem of innovation (UK Innovate, EducateAI, CATS AI) to position the Commonwealth as the global leader in autonomous, sovereign cyberinfrastructure.

## 2. Intellectual Merit and Research Innovation

### 2.1 The Complexity Crisis in Programmable Cyberinfrastructure

FABRIC treats the "network as part of the computing continuum" [Baldin et al., 2020], supporting stateful architectures, in-network computation via GPUs and FPGAs, and dedicated 100G optical links between supercomputing centers. The FABRIC Across Borders (FAB) extension, led in part by Dr. Fei, connects this infrastructure internationally to CERN (Geneva), the University of Tokyo, and the University of Amsterdam [UKNow, 2023].

This programmability introduces a fundamental challenge: **distributed system entropy**. As the number of configurable parameters grows exponentially across sites, traditional software—static, imperative, and open-loop—becomes a bottleneck. Dr. Fei has noted that "science is fast outgrowing the capabilities of today's internet infrastructure" [UKNow, 2023]. The "sliver" and "slice" model of FABRIC, where resources can "grow or shrink, adding or shedding resources under programmatic control" [Baldin et al., 2020], demands a programming paradigm that is itself adaptive.

### 7.3 Compliance Gate Architecture

The `osiris_license.py` module (590 lines) implements automated compliance verification:

- Detects 13+ corporate domain suffixes and 20+ corporate environment variables
- Classifies deployments as Individual, Academic, Corporate, or Government
- Upon detection of unauthorized corporate use, generates a timestamped compliance report archived immutably to Zenodo
- All collected metadata is anonymized (hostname hash, domain class only — no PII)

This architecture ensures the University's intellectual property is shielded from unauthorized industrial exfiltration while permitting unrestricted academic research.
