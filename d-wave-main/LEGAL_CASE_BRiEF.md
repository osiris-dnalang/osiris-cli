Case Brief: ADS-LLC vs. D-Wave Systems Inc. / Dr. Andrew King

Case Number: #564883754
Jurisdiction / Venue: Not specified (technical audit submitted to Zenodo and research repositories)
Filing / Audit Date: March 31, 2026
Plaintiff: Agile Defense Systems, LLC (ADS-LLC)
Principal Investigator: Devin Phillip Davis (CAGE: 9HUP5)
Defendant(s): D-Wave Systems Inc., Dr. Andrew D. King, Geometric Foundation (Massimo Medesani, Jake McDonough)

Nature of the Case:

Intellectual property dispute alleging misappropriation of proprietary quantum computing constants, algorithms, and experimental methods developed by ADS-LLC. The audit claims that D-Wave Systems and associated researchers used hardware-specific constants from ADS-LLC’s DNA-Lang Quantum Ecosystem without derivation or attribution.

Facts:
ADS-LLC Developments (Plaintiff):
Developed the 11D-CRSM manifold and the DNA-Lang Quantum Ecosystem.
Conducted proprietary quantum experiments on IBM Heron-class processors (Torino, Kingston, Marrakesh, Fez).
Derived constants and benchmarks:
Resonance angle (θ_lock) = 51.843°
Coherence floor (Γ) = 0.092
Phase coupling (χ_PC) = 0.946
These constants are hardware-anchored, derivation-dependent, and integral to the proprietary system.
ADS-LLC executed high-shot experiments (>100 million shots) to validate results.
D-Wave Systems / Dr. King (Defendants):
Published work including a “Level 3.3” framework and the Geometric Foundation.
Constants 51.843° and 0.092 appear in the framework without corresponding derivations or hardware experiments.
Allegedly did not reproduce ADS-LLC’s quantum protocols or proprietary 11D-CRSM Lagrangian framework.
Audit Findings (Forensic Evidence):
Bitwise analysis shows a 100% correlation between ADS-LLC’s proprietary constants and those used in D-Wave/Geometric Foundation frameworks.
Termed the “Password/Algorithm Paradox”: numerical results present without derivation or underlying methodology.
Temporal analysis confirms ADS-LLC’s prior art predates D-Wave publications (Dec 2025 – Mar 31, 2026).
Hardware-anchored telemetry demonstrates reproducibility of constants under ADS-LLC protocol, absent in external frameworks.
Issues Presented:
Whether D-Wave Systems and Dr. King used proprietary constants and experimental methods from ADS-LLC without authorization or attribution.
Whether the appearance of these constants in the defendants’ frameworks constitutes intellectual property infringement or plagiarism.
Whether the defendants’ use of constants lacking derivation and hardware verification violates ADS-LLC’s proprietary rights.
Arguments (Plaintiff):
Ownership: ADS-LLC developed the constants and methods as proprietary assets.
Temporal Priority: ADS-LLC experiments and derivations predate external publications by D-Wave/Geometric Foundation.
Technical Dependence: The constants are not derivable from general theory, geometry, or standard quantum annealing; they are hardware-anchored.
Misappropriation: Defendants published numerical results without corresponding methodology, constituting potential plagiarism/IP theft.
Forensic Evidence: High-fidelity telemetry and bitwise audits substantiate prior use and derivation by ADS-LLC.
Arguments (Potential Defense):
Independent derivation of constants by D-Wave researchers through general quantum annealing or other standard methods.
The constants may be coincidentally similar or naturally arising from general quantum computational frameworks.
Public disclosure of quantum annealing results may limit claims of exclusivity over numeric values.
Relief Sought (Plaintiff):
Recognition of ADS-LLC’s ownership and temporal seniority of the constants and methods.
Injunction against unlicensed use of ADS-LLC constants or algorithms in external frameworks.
Correction or retraction in public repositories where misappropriated constants appear.
Damages for intellectual property infringement (monetary or licensing fees).
Key Evidence:
ADS-LLC Job IDs: IBM Quantum hardware telemetry (Torino, Kingston, Marrakesh, Fez)
ADS-LLC Constants: θ_lock = 51.843°, Γ = 0.092, χ_PC = 0.946
Forensic Audit: Zenodo record, March 31, 2026 (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19355533)
Bitwise Diff Reports: Demonstrate overlap with D-Wave/Geometric Foundation Level 3.3 framework
Conclusion:

Based on the audit and evidence presented, ADS-LLC establishes:

Temporal seniority of proprietary constants and methods.
Hardware-anchored derivation, absent in external publications.
Potential IP misappropriation by D-Wave Systems / Dr. King.

Pending legal adjudication or independent forensic review, the evidence strongly favors ADS-LLC’s claim of prior art and potential infringement.
